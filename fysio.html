Fysio

Development, Re-Design

Fysio is a large chain of over 50 hospitals located in Denmark. One of their clinics reached out for a development job that included a website re-design and optimization. The job was pretty simple and streight forward with a small timeframe.

Client
FysioDanmark [DK]
Development
Web, Mobile
Year
2019
Timeframe
2 Weeks

General Development

I was working back to back with the development team of Ulties which made the overall working flow quite nice and easy to manage. The design files were provided by Fysio, which were made by a third party design agency, so we were basically just following a general design roadmap. We didn't have much space for design improvement other than some minor alterations. All design changes were negotiated with Fysio.

Framework

The Framework of choice was pre defined, Fysio were using a wordpress framework. We had to continue using the pre existing framework and do general improvements on it. Given the website sturcture, wordpress was a great choie to begin with.

The only thing we had to edit throughout the entire mobile layout was the general typography sizes, which wasn't any hard to do at all.

Challenges

The mobile design is nothing too complex or mind boggling. It is minimal, slick and clean. No elements were disabled when switching to mobile overview. Nothing much can be said about the mobile version, other than it being extremely smooth and responsive, being coded with slick and smooth animations.

The only thing we had to edit throughout the entire mobile layout was the general typography sizes, which wasn't any hard to do at all.

Branding

The Branding is not something that I made myself. The logo was provided to us by a third party designer but was further changed by me, because the original logo was too much similar to the beats logo. The logo's font is Helvetica Nuene, whih is a verry strong and quality font. It is the Letter a, lower case with some adjustments and curveture, and the follow up text is the same font.

Helvetica Nuene is super similar to Inter, which makes the logo blend with the general typography choice very well.
