Uptime Monitor

UI/UX Development, Branding & Identity, Marketing

Uptime Monitor is a new SASS app that provides website monitoring, SSL checks and a whole lot of more features. Its designed to keep track of live websites for small and large businesses alike, with full automation and ease of use.

Client
Ulties [DK]
Development
Web, Mobile, UI/UX, Branding, Marketing
Year
2019
Timeframe
1 Month

Behind the Concept

Uptime monitor is a small and nifty tool that helps companies, small businesses, and individuals track their website’s online activity. The tool aims to monitor the general health of a website, covering a wide range of checks and providing plenty of features to the end-user. Everything was designed and developed with a priority placed on user accessibility and ease of use. We were aiming to create an everlasting tool that is technically and visually satisfying to use.

The Task

The general task was to create a user-friendly tool that provides a wide range of features in a package that is easy to be used by companies and individuals alike.

Design Language

Uptime Monitor uses a clean and minimal design language system orientated towards minimalism and fast user-friendly UX to provide a prioritized response rate, rather than visual aesthetics.

UI/UX

We had to go very simple and clean on this project, so we could ensure that the user experience was focused on productivity. This pushed us to cut down on vibrant colors and large scale artwork elements.

Menu Style

Uptime is a project that’s jam-packed with features. One of the hardest tasks for us was to ensure that things were organized and simple. The initial menu idea was to have subcategories on different pages, but later on, we moved to a more complex menu system that ensured great User Experience and an uncluttered feel to the general layout of all the different pages.

Typography

The Typography font choice had to be something unorthodox. Typically I hate working with the basic fonts like Poppins and Roboto, although in some cases they make an amazing presentation, this time the font of choice was a freemium font.

Also, I must not that the design's fundamental is actually based on Typography as a means of visual representation.

For typography we chose Inter. Inter features a tall x-height to aid in readability of mixed-case and lower-case text. Several OpenType features are provided as well, like contextual alternates that adjusts punctuation depending on the shape of surrounding glyphs, slashed zero for when you need to disambiguate “0” from “o”, tabular numbers, etc.

The overall fusion of dark deep colors and the crisp and clean visual aesthetic of Inter made an amazing fusion that blend in the final product.

Branding

The Branding is not something that I made myself. The logo was provided to us by a third party designer but was further changed by me, because the original logo was too much similar to the beats logo. The logo's font is Helvetica Nuene, whih is a verry strong and quality font. It is the Letter a, lower case with some adjustments and curveture, and the follow up text is the same font.

Helvetica Nuene is super similar to Inter, which makes the logo blend with the general typography choice very well.
